## Key Quotes

**On converged outcomes:**
> Bootcamp placement (71-79%) ≈ CS degree placement (68%); 72% of employers see bootcamp grads as equally prepared

**On false precision:**
> Creating 18+ segment cells with fuzzy boundaries gives false confidence. Admitting "we can't predict 2030, here are robust strategies" is more honest and actionable.

**On class stratification:**
> Guidance will effectively say "If you can access elite schools, go; if not, do hybrid/bootcamp," which maps wealth to educational path. This is descriptively accurate. Pretending otherwise doesn't help decision-makers.

**On the hybrid path:**
> Every analysis assumes "degree XOR bootcamp" when the data suggests "any bachelor's + self-study + bootcamp" may offer superior risk-adjusted returns.

**On execution vs selection:**
> Within any category, individual variation is massive. A motivated bootcamp grad who networks, builds portfolio, and contributes to open source vastly outperforms an unmotivated CS degree holder.
