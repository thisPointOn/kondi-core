# Remote-First Work Policy (Path A)

## Policy Statement

**Effective Date**: [Insert Date]  
**Policy Owner**: [Chief People Officer / Head of HR]  
**Review Cycle**: Annual (every 12 months)

[Organization Name] operates as a **remote-first** organization. This means:

1. **Default location is remote**: Employees work from their chosen location unless a specific role requires physical presence
2. **Async communication is primary**: Work is designed to happen asynchronously across time zones
3. **In-person is intentional**: We gather in person for specific purposes (onboarding, team building, strategic planning), not as a default
4. **Equal opportunity regardless of location**: Career growth, compensation, and access to information do not depend on proximity to an office

This policy applies to all employees unless explicitly exempted (see Eligibility section below).

---

## Philosophy and Principles

### Why Remote-First

We chose remote-first to:
- **Access global talent**: Hire the best people regardless of location
- **Enable deep work**: Reduce synchronous interruptions and meeting overhead
- **Support flexibility**: Allow employees to design work around their lives, not vice versa
- **Build inclusive systems**: Create explicit processes that work for everyone, not just those who can attend impromptu meetings

### What Remote-First Is Not

Remote-first is not:
- ❌ **"Work from anywhere, do anything"**: We have clear expectations for communication, documentation, and performance
- ❌ **Zero in-person interaction**: We invest in intentional in-person gatherings
- ❌ **Isolation**: We build connection through structured interaction, not proximity
- ❌ **Cost-cutting**: This is about accessing talent and enabling focus, not reducing office expenses

### Core Principles

1. **Documentation over conversation**: If it's important, write it down
2. **Async over sync**: Default to asynchronous communication; use meetings intentionally
3. **Output over activity**: We measure results delivered, not hours worked or responsiveness
4. **Inclusive by default**: Processes work for all time zones and communication styles
5. **Explicit over implicit**: We don't rely on "you should know this" or hallway knowledge

## Eligibility and Exceptions

### Eligible Roles

All roles are remote-eligible unless they require physical presence for operational reasons.

**Eligible**: Engineering, product, design, marketing, sales, customer success, data, HR, finance, recruiting, legal, executive leadership

**Evaluation criteria**: Can this role's core responsibilities be performed effectively with async collaboration and periodic in-person gatherings?

### Roles Requiring Physical Presence

The following roles may require regular office presence:
- **Facilities management**: Managing physical offices/equipment
- **Lab work**: Roles requiring specialized physical equipment
- **Roles with legal requirements**: Positions where regulations mandate physical presence
- **Security-sensitive roles**: Where remote work creates unacceptable compliance risk

These roles will be clearly marked in job descriptions as "Office-Based" with required office days specified.

### Geographic Requirements

- **No geographic restrictions** for roles without specific timezone requirements
- **Timezone overlap requirements**: Roles requiring real-time collaboration with specific teams must have ≥4 hours of business hours overlap with their primary collaborators
- **Country restrictions**: We hire in [list countries where you have legal entities]. Expanding to new countries requires legal/tax setup (6-12 month process)
- **Travel requirements**: Some roles require quarterly or monthly in-person attendance (specified in job description)

### Accommodations

Employees who require office access for disability accommodations, home environment constraints, or other documented needs will have access to office space or co-working stipends.

**Process**: Request accommodations through [HR contact/system] with documentation if applicable. Approved within 5 business days.

## Organizational Commitments

Remote-first succeeds when the organization builds supporting infrastructure. We commit to:

### 1. Documentation Infrastructure

- **Central knowledge base**: All company information lives in [Notion/Confluence/Wiki] with clear organization and search
- **Documentation ownership**: Every critical process has an assigned owner who keeps it current
- **Documentation culture**: Managers model creating and maintaining documentation
- **Onboarding documentation**: Complete self-service onboarding guides for all roles

**Timeline**: Documentation infrastructure in place within 6 months of policy adoption

### 2. Async Communication Norms

- **Default response time**: 24 business hours for most communication (urgent items marked explicitly)
- **Meeting necessity criteria**: Written guidelines for when meetings are required vs. async discussion
- **Recording and notes**: All meetings recorded or documented with notes posted within 24 hours
- **Timezone consideration**: Meetings rotated to distribute inconvenience or recorded for async viewing

**Training**: All employees complete async communication training within first 30 days

### 3. Bias Mitigation Systems

Remote work creates new bias risks (timezone bias, communication style bias, "always-on" culture bias). We commit to:

- **Promotion/compensation tracking**: Monitor promotion rates, compensation changes, and performance ratings by location, timezone, and demographic group
- **Quarterly bias audits**: Review outcomes every quarter; investigate disparities >10 percentage points
- **Inclusive meeting practices**: Enforce practices that level the playing field for distributed participants
- **Output-based evaluation**: Performance evaluations based on measurable results, not visibility or responsiveness

**Accountability**: People/HR team publishes quarterly bias metrics to company

### 4. Equipment and Connectivity

All remote employees receive:
- **Home office stipend**: $[1500-2500] one-time setup + $[500-1000] annual refresh
- **Internet reimbursement**: Up to $[75-150]/month for business-class internet
- **Co-working stipend**: $[200-300]/month for employees who need workspace outside home
- **Travel to office**: Flights + accommodation for required in-person gatherings (onboarding, team offsites, all-hands)

**Hardware**: Company-provided laptop, monitor, keyboard, mouse, headset (standard for role)

### 5. Intentional In-Person Gatherings

We bring people together in person for specific purposes:

- **Onboarding**: All new hires attend 3-5 day in-person onboarding within first 60 days
- **Team offsites**: Each team meets in person 1-2x per year for planning and relationship building
- **All-hands**: Full company gathering 1-2x per year
- **Department gatherings**: Function-specific gatherings (e.g., engineering offsites, sales kickoffs) as needed

**Budget**: Each employee allocated $[X] annually for in-person travel/gatherings

**Inclusivity**: In-person gatherings scheduled ≥60 days in advance to accommodate caregiving, visa needs, and other constraints

## Employee Expectations and Responsibilities

### Work Location Requirements

- **Primary work location**: Employee chooses location; must update HR when location changes
- **Workspace standards**: Employees responsible for maintaining workspace with:
  - Reliable high-speed internet (minimum 25 Mbps download, 5 Mbps upload)
  - Quiet space for video calls
  - Ergonomic setup (see equipment stipend above)
- **Work-from-anywhere**: Employees may work from locations other than primary address temporarily (e.g., visiting family) with manager notification if >5 consecutive days

### Communication Expectations

- **Response time**: Respond to messages within 24 business hours unless on PTO or in focus time
- **Availability hours**: Maintain 6 hours of "generally available" time (not in back-to-back meetings) for collaboration
- **Calendar accuracy**: Keep calendar current; block focus time to protect deep work
- **Status indicators**: Use Slack/Teams status to indicate availability (in meeting, focus time, away)
- **Working hours clarity**: Communicate your working hours to your team; document in [location]

### Documentation Responsibilities

- **Document decisions**: After meetings where decisions are made, document outcome + rationale in [knowledge base]
- **Update docs you use**: If you find outdated documentation, either update it or flag it for the owner
- **Write for async**: When posting questions or updates, include sufficient context for people to respond without real-time follow-up

### Meeting Participation

- **Camera on**: Default to video on for meetings to maintain connection (not mandatory if bandwidth/other constraints)
- **Join from own device**: Even if multiple people in same location, join from individual devices for equality
- **Prepare in advance**: Review materials before meetings; add agenda items 24 hours ahead
- **Contribute to notes**: Meetings >30 minutes should have rotating note-taker role

### Performance and Accountability

- **Output focus**: You're evaluated on results delivered, not hours worked or Slack responsiveness
- **Proactive communication**: Communicate blockers, delays, and progress without being asked
- **Own your development**: Career growth requires explicit advocacy; managers can't "see" your work without you sharing it
- **Meet commitments**: Hit deadlines, deliver quality work, communicate if commitments at risk

## Communication Protocols

### Communication Tool Usage

| Tool | Purpose | Expected Response Time |
|------|---------|------------------------|
| [Slack/Teams] | Day-to-day coordination, questions, quick updates | 24 hours (8 hours for urgent) |
| Email | External communication, formal documentation, anything needing audit trail | 48 hours |
| [Project Management Tool] | Work tracking, status updates, deliverable handoffs | Checked daily |
| [Knowledge Base] | Documentation, processes, decisions, onboarding | Updated as needed |
| Video calls | Synchronous collaboration, sensitive discussions, relationship building | Scheduled in advance |

### Urgency Indicators

Use these indicators to signal urgency appropriately:

- **Urgent (needs response within 4 hours)**: Direct message with "URGENT:" prefix + brief context
- **Time-sensitive (needs response within 24 hours)**: @mention in appropriate channel
- **Standard (24-48 hours)**: Normal message, no special flag
- **FYI (no response needed)**: Prefix with "FYI:" or post in designated FYI channels

**Urgent overuse**: If >10% of your messages are marked urgent, you're overusing the signal. Managers will address this in 1-on-1s.

### Meeting Guidelines

**Before scheduling a meeting, ask:**
1. Can this be handled with a document + async comments?
2. Do all invitees need to attend, or can some review a recording?
3. Is there a clear decision or outcome this meeting will produce?

If NO to all three, don't schedule the meeting.

**Meeting best practices:**
- **Agenda required**: All meetings >15 minutes must have agenda posted 24 hours in advance
- **Materials in advance**: Share documents 24 hours before meeting for review
- **Start/end on time**: Respect people's calendars; start at :05 past the hour for breaks
- **Action items**: End meetings with clear action items, owners, and due dates documented
- **Record or document**: Post recording or detailed notes within 24 hours

**Default meeting length**: 25 or 50 minutes (not 30/60) to allow transition time

## Time Zone Management

### Overlap Requirements

- **Core collaboration hours**: Each team defines 3-4 hour "overlap window" when most team members are available
- **Flexibility outside core hours**: Employees manage their remaining hours based on their preferences and workload

### Meeting Scheduling

- **Rotate meeting times**: For distributed teams, rotate meeting times monthly to distribute inconvenience
- **Timezone labeling**: All meeting invites include timezone (use UTC or specify timezone explicitly)
- **No permanent disadvantage**: Monitor whether specific individuals/timezones consistently get inconvenient meeting times; address through rotation

### Async-First for Distributed Teams

Teams with >6 hour timezone spread default to:
- **Weekly async updates** instead of daily standups
- **Recorded presentations** instead of real-time all-hands
- **Document collaboration** with async comments instead of brainstorming meetings

## Career Development and Promotion

Remote-first requires explicit career development practices since informal mentorship and visibility don't happen organically.

### Performance Evaluation Criteria

Employees are evaluated on:
1. **Results delivered**: Measurable outcomes against goals
2. **Collaboration quality**: How well you work with others (documentation, communication, knowledge sharing)
3. **Growth and learning**: Skills developed, feedback incorporated
4. **Cultural contribution**: How you strengthen team culture and live company values

**Explicitly NOT evaluated on:**
- ❌ Slack responsiveness or "always-on" availability
- ❌ Timezone (working hours aligned with headquarters)
- ❌ Participation in optional social events
- ❌ Frequency of office visits (for roles where office visits are optional)

### Promotion Process

Promotion decisions follow a structured rubric:
- **Documented criteria**: Each level has explicit criteria for skills, scope, and impact
- **Evidence-based**: Promotion packets include specific examples of work meeting next-level criteria
- **Calibration**: Promotion decisions calibrated across teams to ensure consistency
- **Bias monitoring**: Promotion rates tracked by location, timezone, and demographic group; disparities investigated

**Timeline**: Promotion cycles occur [biannually/annually] with decisions announced within 2 weeks of cycle close.

### Mentorship and Sponsorship

Since mentorship doesn't happen organically in remote environments:
- **Formal mentorship program**: Employees can request mentors matched by function/career goals
- **Sponsorship for underrepresented groups**: Senior leaders assigned to sponsor high-potential employees from underrepresented groups
- **Career development conversations**: Managers required to have quarterly career development discussions (separate from performance reviews)

### Visibility Equity

To ensure remote employees have equal visibility:
- **Brag documents**: Employees maintain running documents of accomplishments, shared in promotion/review cycles
- **Project showcases**: Regular (monthly) presentations where teams share work with broader organization
- **Cross-functional rotation**: Opportunities to work on cross-functional projects to build visibility across teams

## Security and Compliance

### Data Security Requirements

All employees must:
- **Use VPN**: Connect via company VPN when accessing internal systems
- **Encrypt devices**: Enable full-disk encryption on all company devices
- **Secure wifi**: Use WPA2/WPA3 encrypted wifi networks (no open/public wifi for sensitive work)
- **Lock screens**: Auto-lock after 5 minutes of inactivity
- **Secure physical environment**: Ensure screens not visible to others during video calls with sensitive information

### Prohibited Practices

- ❌ Accessing company systems from shared/public computers
- ❌ Storing company data on personal devices/accounts
- ❌ Discussing confidential information in public spaces where conversations can be overheard
- ❌ Sharing login credentials or API keys

### Compliance Monitoring

For roles handling sensitive data (customer data, financial information, etc.):
- **Enhanced security requirements**: May require additional measures (dedicated network, secure room, periodic audits)
- **Access logging**: All access to sensitive systems logged and audited
- **Annual compliance training**: Required for all employees handling regulated data

## Policy Violations and Enforcement

### Common Violations

This policy is violated when employees:
- Consistently miss the 24-hour response time without setting proper status/away messages
- Don't document decisions or processes as required
- Fail to maintain secure work environment (VPN usage, encryption)
- Work from a country where we don't have legal authorization to employ them
- Consistently mark non-urgent items as urgent

### Enforcement Process

1. **First violation**: Manager coaching conversation; documented in 1-on-1 notes
2. **Second violation**: Formal warning; performance improvement plan if pattern continues
3. **Continued violations**: Standard performance management process up to termination

**Security violations**: Immediate escalation to Security team; may result in immediate account suspension
