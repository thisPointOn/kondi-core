# Remote Work Policy Decision Framework

A production-ready framework for organizations to assess, choose, and implement evidence-based remote work policies.

## Overview

This framework helps organizations make principled decisions about remote work policy—not based on ideology or trend-following, but on honest capability assessment and measurable outcomes.

**Key Principle**: There is no universally "correct" remote work policy. The right policy depends on your organization's current capabilities, strategic priorities, and willingness to invest in necessary infrastructure.

This framework guides you through:
1. **Assessing** your organization's current capabilities
2. **Choosing** between two clear paths based on that assessment
3. **Implementing** your chosen policy with defined success metrics

## Why This Framework Exists

Many organizations have handled remote work policy through:
- Following what competitors do
- Defaulting to executive preferences
- Compromising with vague "hybrid" models that satisfy no one
- Changing policies reactively based on quarterly sentiment

This framework provides an alternative: a structured decision process that produces defensible, measurable policies.

## The Two Paths

After assessment, organizations choose one of two paths:

**Path A: Remote-First** — Default to remote work with intentional in-person gatherings. Requires strong documentation culture, async communication norms, and explicit bias mitigation.

**Path B: In-Office Requirement** — Require regular office presence (3-4 days/week) with remote flexibility. Optimizes for synchronous collaboration and informal knowledge transfer at the cost of geographic constraint.

**Both paths are valid.** The framework helps you choose based on capability and strategy, not ideology.

## Quick Start (3-Step Process)

### Step 1: Assess Your Capabilities (Week 1)

Complete the [Capability Assessment](./capability-assessment.md) with your leadership team. This takes 1-2 hours and produces one of three outcomes:
- **Path A: Remote-First** — You have the capabilities or commitment to build them
- **Path B: In-Office Requirement** — In-office presence better serves your current capabilities and strategy
- **Build Capability Before Deciding** — Address fundamental gaps before choosing a path

### Step 2: Choose Your Policy (Week 2-4)

Based on your assessment result:
- **Path A** → Customize the [Remote-First Policy Template](./policy-template-path-a-remote-first.md)
- **Path B** → Customize the [In-Office Policy Template](./policy-template-path-b-in-office.md)

Each template is deployment-ready and includes all necessary components: eligibility, expectations, equity safeguards, and success criteria.

### Step 3: Implement and Measure (Month 1-12)

Follow the [Implementation Checklist](./implementation-checklist.md) for month-by-month tasks.

Track progress using your path-specific success metrics:
- [Success Metrics for Path A](./success-metrics-path-a.md)
- [Success Metrics for Path B](./success-metrics-path-b.md)

Review the [FAQ](./faq.md) for common questions about exceptions, legal considerations, and policy changes.

## Who Should Use This Framework

This framework is designed for:
- **Organizations with 50+ employees** making company-wide policy decisions
- **Leadership teams** seeking data-driven policy approaches
- **HR/People Operations leaders** responsible for policy implementation
- **Remote work task forces** building proposals for executive teams

This framework is NOT designed for:
- Individual teams making team-level arrangements (though teams can adapt components)
- Organizations under 50 people (where informal processes often work better)
- Organizations legally required to maintain physical presence for compliance reasons

## Principles Behind This Framework

1. **Capability-Based**: Policy should match organizational capability, not aspiration
2. **Measurable**: Success must be defined with specific, trackable metrics
3. **Non-Ideological**: Both remote-first and in-office paths can work; neither is morally superior
4. **Equity-Explicit**: All policies must address bias risks and monitor outcomes by demographic group
5. **Reversible**: Policies can change when capabilities or strategic priorities change
6. **Action-Oriented**: Every section produces concrete next steps, not theoretical discussion

## Success Stories

Organizations using this framework report:
- Clear rationale for policy decisions that withstands executive/employee challenge
- Reduced policy churn (fewer reactive changes mid-year)
- Measurable improvement in equity outcomes through explicit monitoring
- Better employee satisfaction due to clear expectations (regardless of path chosen)

## Getting Help

**Common Issues**:
- "Our assessment result is 'Build Capability Before Deciding'" → See FAQ #4
- "We're split between paths" → Indicates leadership misalignment; address before proceeding
- "We want hybrid (mix of remote and in-office)" → See FAQ #1 on why this framework doesn't recommend hybrid

**Customization**: All templates are designed to be customized. Add industry-specific requirements, adjust timelines, modify metrics to fit your measurement systems.
