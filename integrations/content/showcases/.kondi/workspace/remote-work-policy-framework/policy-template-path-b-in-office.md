# In-Office Work Policy (Path B)

## Policy Statement

**Effective Date**: [Insert Date]  
**Policy Owner**: [Chief People Officer / Head of HR]  
**Review Cycle**: Annual (every 12 months)

[Organization Name] operates with an **in-office requirement** model. This means:

1. **Default location is office**: Employees work from company offices for the majority of their work week
2. **Scheduled office days**: Employees are required to be in the office [3-4] days per week, with specific anchor days
3. **Remote flexibility**: Employees may work remotely [1-2] days per week and have flexibility for occasional remote work needs
4. **Synchronous collaboration**: We optimize for real-time collaboration, informal knowledge sharing, and team cohesion through physical presence

This policy applies to all employees unless explicitly exempted (see Eligibility section below).

---

## Philosophy and Principles

### Why In-Office

We chose an in-office requirement model to:
- **Enable synchronous collaboration**: Facilitate rapid iteration, brainstorming, and real-time problem-solving
- **Support knowledge transfer**: Enable informal mentorship and learning through proximity
- **Build team cohesion**: Create shared culture and relationships through regular in-person interaction
- **Optimize for our work**: Our work style benefits from high-frequency collaboration and quick feedback loops

### What In-Office Requirement Is Not

This policy is not:
- ❌ **"Remote workers are less productive"**: This is about optimizing for our specific work needs, not judging remote work
- ❌ **Micromanagement**: We trust employees to manage their time; office presence is about collaboration, not surveillance
- ❌ **One-size-fits-all**: We provide accommodations for those who need remote work for legitimate reasons
- ❌ **No flexibility**: We offer remote work days and flexible hours within the framework

### Core Principles

1. **Collaboration over isolation**: Physical presence enables better teamwork for our specific needs
2. **Equity through structure**: Clear rules applied consistently prevent favoritism and proximity bias
3. **Flexibility within framework**: Remote days and flexible hours available within office requirement
4. **Outcomes over hours**: We measure results, not time in seat
5. **Intentional office design**: Office space designed for collaboration, not cubicle farms

## Office Schedule Requirements

### Required Office Days

**Standard requirement**: Employees work from the office **[3-4] days per week** (specify based on your choice).

**Anchor days**: All employees must be in office on:
- **[Tuesday and Thursday]** (adjust to your company needs)
- **Plus [1-2] additional days** chosen by team/individual

**Rationale for anchor days**: Ensures team overlap for collaboration while preserving individual flexibility.

### Team-Specific Schedules

Individual teams may establish additional in-office expectations based on their work:
- Teams may designate all [5] days in-office if work requires it
- Teams may specify which [3-4] days are required beyond anchor days
- Changes to team schedules require [manager + HR] approval

**Team schedules must**:
- Be documented and communicated ≥30 days in advance
- Apply equally to all team members (no individual exceptions without formal accommodation)
- Be reviewed quarterly for equity impact

### Remote Work Days

Employees may work remotely [1-2] days per week on non-anchor days.

**Remote day guidelines**:
- Communicate your remote days to your team weekly (update shared calendar)
- Remote days should be used for focus work, async tasks, or meetings that don't require physical presence
- You're still expected to be fully available and productive on remote days
- Excessive last-minute remote day changes may be addressed by managers

### Flexibility for Occasional Remote Work

Beyond scheduled remote days, employees may request occasional remote work for:
- Appointments (medical, childcare, etc.)
- Home maintenance/deliveries
- Weather or transportation disruptions
- Focus work requiring extended quiet time

**Process**: Notify manager via [Slack/email/system] with >24 hours notice when possible. Approval assumed unless manager responds otherwise.

**Limit**: Up to [4-6] additional remote days per month beyond scheduled remote days (adjust based on office day requirement).

## Eligibility and Exceptions

### Eligible for Standard Policy

All employees in roles that can be performed from office locations are subject to this policy.

**Geographic hiring constraint**: We hire employees within commutable distance of our offices:
- **Commutable distance defined as**: ≤[60 minutes / 50 miles] one-way during non-peak hours
- **Verified during hiring**: Candidates confirm commute feasibility before offer acceptance

### Roles Requiring Full-Time Office Presence

Some roles require 5 days/week office presence:
- **Facilities and office management**: Require daily physical presence to manage office operations
- **Lab or hardware roles**: Require access to specialized equipment
- **Roles with compliance requirements**: Where regulations mandate in-person presence
- **Receptionist/office coordinator**: Customer-facing roles requiring physical presence

These roles are marked "Office-Based, 5 Days Required" in job descriptions.

### Accommodation Requests

We provide accommodations for employees who cannot meet the office requirement due to:

**Disability accommodations**:
- Process: Request through [HR/Accommodations contact] with medical documentation
- Review: Case-by-case evaluation; interactive process to determine reasonable accommodation
- May result in: Reduced office days, hybrid schedule, or full-time remote arrangement

**Temporary life circumstances**:
- Medical recovery (own or family member)
- Temporary caregiving responsibilities
- Pregnancy complications
- Other documented temporary needs

**Process**: Submit request to [HR contact] with supporting documentation. Decision within 5 business days; temporary accommodations granted for specified period with review.

**Long-term remote arrangements**:
- Employees who permanently relocate beyond commutable distance may be eligible for remote work if:
  - Performance is strong (meets/exceeds expectations)
  - Role can be performed remotely effectively
  - Manager and HR approve
- This is granted as exception, not entitlement; requires formal approval process

## Equity Safeguards and Monitoring

In-office policies create proximity bias risk. We commit to active monitoring and mitigation:

### Promotion and Compensation Tracking

**What we monitor**:
- Promotion rates by office vs. remote days per week
- Compensation changes by frequency of office attendance
- Performance ratings by office presence
- Access to high-visibility projects by location

**Review cadence**: Quarterly analysis by People/HR team

**Action threshold**: If employees working minimum required days are promoted/compensated at rates >10 percentage points below employees with maximum office presence, we investigate and address.

### Preventing "Face-Time" Culture

**Explicitly prohibited**:
- ❌ Managers commenting positively on employees being in office beyond required days
- ❌ Scheduling important discussions only on non-anchor days when some employees may be remote
- ❌ Using office attendance as performance signal ("Sarah's always here, she must be dedicated")
- ❌ Rewarding employees for voluntary office attendance beyond requirement

**Required practices**:
- ✅ Important discussions scheduled on anchor days when everyone is present
- ✅ Performance evaluations based on output and results, not office presence
- ✅ Equal access to projects and opportunities regardless of whether employee does 3 or 5 days in office

### Remote Day Equality

Employees using their allocated remote days cannot face penalties:
- Performance reviews will not reference remote day usage (unless below required minimum)
- Promotion decisions will not consider whether employee uses remote days
- Team culture must treat remote days as fully acceptable, not requiring justification

**Manager training**: All managers complete "Managing Hybrid Teams" training covering bias awareness and inclusive practices.

### Addressing Disparities

If monitoring reveals disparities:

1. **Investigate root cause**: Is there actual bias, or are there confounding factors?
2. **Develop action plan**: Specific interventions to address identified issues
3. **Communicate transparently**: Share findings and remediation plan with affected groups
4. **Track progress**: Monthly check-ins until disparity resolved

## Communication and Collaboration Expectations

### Communication Tools

| Tool | Purpose | Expected Response Time |
|------|---------|------------------------|
| In-person | Complex discussions, brainstorming, relationship building | Real-time when in office |
| [Slack/Teams] | Quick questions, coordination, real-time discussion | 2 hours during work hours |
| Email | External communication, formal documentation, async updates | 24 hours |
| [Project Management] | Work tracking, status updates | Checked daily |
| Video calls | Meetings when some participants are remote | Scheduled in advance |

### Meeting Guidelines

**In-person meeting default**: On anchor days when full team is present, default to in-person meetings.

**Hybrid meeting best practices**:
When some participants are remote:
- Use video conferencing even if majority is in office
- Consider having everyone join from their own device (even if in same office) for equality
- Share materials in advance for remote participants
- Monitor chat for questions from remote participants
- Verbally summarize visual materials for remote participants

**Required practices**:
- Meeting agendas posted ≥24 hours in advance
- Notes documented for anyone who couldn't attend
- Action items clearly assigned with owners and due dates

### Work Hours and Availability

**Core hours**: [10am-4pm local time] when employees are generally available for collaboration

**Flexibility**: Employees can flex start/end times within reason (e.g., 7am-3pm or 10am-6pm) with manager awareness

**Remote day availability**: On remote days, employees must be fully available during core hours (not running errands, caring for children without childcare, etc.)

## Career Development and Promotion

### Performance Evaluation Criteria

Employees are evaluated on:
1. **Results delivered**: Measurable outcomes against goals (weighted 60%)
2. **Collaboration quality**: How effectively you work with others (weighted 20%)
3. **Growth and learning**: Skills developed, feedback incorporated (weighted 10%)
4. **Cultural contribution**: Living company values, helping others (weighted 10%)

**Explicitly NOT evaluated on:**
- ❌ Total days in office beyond the required minimum
- ❌ Being first to arrive or last to leave
- ❌ Attendance at optional social events
- ❌ Visibility to executives through office presence

### Promotion Process

**Structured process**:
- **Criteria**: Each level has documented criteria for skills, scope, and impact
- **Evidence-based**: Promotion packets include specific examples of work meeting criteria
- **Calibration**: Decisions calibrated across teams for consistency
- **Bias monitoring**: Rates tracked by office attendance patterns and demographic groups

**Timeline**: Promotion cycles [biannually/annually] with decisions within 2 weeks of cycle close.

**Transparency**: Promotion criteria published; employees can self-assess readiness

### Mentorship and Growth

**Formal programs**:
- **Mentorship matching**: Employees can request mentors; matched quarterly
- **Leadership development**: Programs for high-potential employees regardless of office attendance
- **Lunch and learns**: Regular learning sessions scheduled on anchor days for full participation

**Manager responsibilities**:
- Quarterly career development discussions (separate from performance reviews)
- Connecting direct reports with cross-functional opportunities
- Nominating strong performers for high-visibility projects (monitored for bias)

## Security and Compliance

### Office Security

- **Badge access**: All employees receive building/floor access badge
- **Visitor policy**: Guests must sign in and be escorted
- **Clean desk policy**: Lock sensitive materials when away from desk
- **Device security**: Lock screens when stepping away

### Remote Day Security

When working remotely:
- **VPN required**: Connect via company VPN for all internal systems access
- **Secure wifi**: Use WPA2/WPA3 encrypted networks (not public wifi)
- **Screen privacy**: Ensure screens not visible to others in public spaces
- **No public computers**: Never access company systems from shared/public computers

### Data Handling

- **Sensitive data**: Customer data, financial info, and regulated data only accessed on company networks (office or VPN)
- **Document storage**: All company documents in company cloud storage, not personal devices
- **Printing**: Minimize printing; sensitive documents printed and disposed of securely

## Policy Violations and Enforcement

### Attendance Tracking

We track office attendance through:
- [Badge swipe data / desk booking system / team attestation]

**Purpose**: Ensure policy compliance and identify patterns needing support

**Not used for**: Performance evaluation beyond meeting minimum requirement

### Common Violations

Policy violations include:
- Consistently not meeting required office days without approved accommodation
- Working from non-commutable location without approval
- Misrepresenting remote day usage (saying you're working from home but actually unavailable)
- Security policy violations (no VPN, public wifi for sensitive work)

### Progressive Discipline

1. **First instance**: Manager conversation; understanding of policy confirmed
2. **Pattern (2-3 instances in 3 months)**: Formal warning; documented in performance file
3. **Continued non-compliance**: Performance improvement plan
4. **Persistent non-compliance**: Up to termination

**Extenuating circumstances**: Life happens. Communicate with your manager if you're struggling to meet requirements due to temporary circumstances.
