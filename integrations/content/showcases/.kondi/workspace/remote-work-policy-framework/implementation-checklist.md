# Implementation Checklist

## Purpose

This month-by-month implementation plan guides you through deploying your chosen remote work policy (Path A or Path B) over 12 months.

**How to use this checklist**:
- Complete Month 1 tasks before announcing your policy decision
- Months 2-6 focus on building infrastructure (different tracks for Path A vs. Path B)
- Months 7-12 focus on measurement, iteration, and refinement
- Check off tasks as you complete them; adjust timelines as needed for your organization

**Key roles**:
- **Executive Sponsor**: [Title] - Overall accountability, decision authority
- **Implementation Lead**: [Name/Title] - Day-to-day project management
- **HR/People Team**: Policy documentation, training, compliance
- **IT/Facilities**: Infrastructure, tools, space design
- **Managers**: Team-level implementation, feedback

---

## Month 1: Assessment and Decision

**Goal**: Complete capability assessment and make path decision with leadership alignment

### Week 1-2: Capability Assessment

- [ ] **Convene assessment team**: Include exec sponsor, HR, IT, representatives from major departments, at least one IC
- [ ] **Complete capability assessment**: Work through [Capability Assessment](./capability-assessment.md) honestly
- [ ] **Review results**: Calculate scores across 6 categories
- [ ] **Identify gaps**: If "Build Capability" result, document specific gaps to address before proceeding
- [ ] **Reach consensus**: Ensure leadership team agrees on assessment results and path recommendation

**Deliverable**: Assessment scorecard with path recommendation

### Week 3: Baseline Data Collection

- [ ] **Collect engagement data**: Run baseline engagement survey (if not recently done)
- [ ] **Document current state**: Attendance patterns, meeting time, collaboration norms, documentation maturity
- [ ] **Gather financial data**: Current office costs, equipment costs, real estate commitments
- [ ] **Review attrition data**: Last 12 months voluntary departures with reasons

**Deliverable**: Baseline metrics dashboard (Month 0 snapshot)

### Week 4: Path Decision and Communication Planning

- [ ] **Make formal path decision**: Executive team commits to Path A or Path B
- [ ] **Draft policy document**: Customize chosen template ([Path A](./policy-template-path-a-remote-first.md) or [Path B](./policy-template-path-b-in-office.md))
- [ ] **Identify policy owner**: Assign executive sponsor and implementation lead
- [ ] **Create communication plan**: How and when will you announce? Who communicates what?
- [ ] **Plan feedback mechanisms**: How will employees provide input during rollout?
- [ ] **Legal review**: Have legal team review policy for compliance, discrimination risk, geographic hiring constraints

**Deliverable**: Customized policy draft, communication plan, project team assigned

---

## Month 2: Communication and Planning

**Goal**: Announce policy, communicate rationale, begin infrastructure planning

### Week 1: Internal Communication

- [ ] **All-hands announcement**: Executive sponsor presents policy decision with rationale (reference assessment data)
- [ ] **Publish policy document**: Make full policy available to all employees
- [ ] **Manager briefing**: Train managers on policy details and how to answer team questions
- [ ] **FAQ publication**: Post [FAQ](./faq.md) and create channel for ongoing questions
- [ ] **Open feedback period**: Create mechanism for employees to ask questions and provide feedback (30-day window)

**Communication principles**: Be transparent about why this path was chosen, acknowledge trade-offs, show data

### Week 2-3: Infrastructure Planning

**Path A (Remote-First) specific tasks**:
- [ ] **Audit documentation systems**: Inventory current knowledge base, identify gaps
- [ ] **Select documentation platform**: If needed, choose wiki/knowledge base tool (Notion, Confluence, etc.)
- [ ] **Identify documentation owners**: Assign owners for critical process documentation
- [ ] **Plan async training**: Design async communication and documentation training for all employees

**Path B (In-Office) specific tasks**:
- [ ] **Office space assessment**: Review current office capacity vs. required attendance
- [ ] **Desk/space allocation plan**: Decide on hot desking vs. assigned desks, team neighborhoods
- [ ] **Anchor day selection**: Confirm anchor days based on team needs (e.g., Tues/Thurs)
- [ ] **Badge/tracking system**: Confirm method for tracking office attendance
- [ ] **Commute support planning**: Identify commuter benefits to offer (transit, parking, etc.)

**Both paths**:
- [ ] **Equipment budget**: Finalize budget for home office stipends, equipment, co-working
- [ ] **IT tool audit**: Review communication tools (Slack, Zoom, etc.) and identify gaps
- [ ] **Training curriculum**: Plan manager and employee training programs
- [ ] **Metrics dashboard setup**: Begin building tracking dashboard for success metrics

### Week 4: Policy Refinement

- [ ] **Review employee feedback**: Analyze questions/concerns from feedback period
- [ ] **Adjust policy**: Make refinements based on legitimate concerns (document what changed and why)
- [ ] **Exception process setup**: Create workflow for accommodation requests
- [ ] **Finalize implementation timeline**: Confirm rollout dates for each phase

**Deliverable**: Final policy document, infrastructure plan, training curriculum outline, metrics dashboard

## Month 3: Infrastructure Build (Path A & B diverge)

**Goal**: Build core infrastructure required for chosen path

### Path A: Remote-First Infrastructure

**Week 1-2: Documentation**
- [ ] **Set up knowledge base**: Configure wiki/documentation platform, create structure
- [ ] **Document critical processes**: Start with top 20 processes (onboarding, deployment, approvals, etc.)
- [ ] **Create documentation templates**: Standard templates for process docs, meeting notes, decision records
- [ ] **Assign documentation owners**: Each critical doc has owner with review schedule

**Week 2-3: Communication Tools**
- [ ] **Configure async tools**: Set up channels, workflows, notification settings for async-first
- [ ] **Establish response time norms**: Configure status messages, auto-responders for 24hr response time
- [ ] **Set up meeting recording**: Enable and test recording for all meeting platforms
- [ ] **Create meeting guidelines**: Post guidelines for meeting necessity, agendas, etc.

**Week 3-4: Home Office Setup**
- [ ] **Process equipment orders**: Set up vendor relationships for home office equipment
- [ ] **Create equipment request workflow**: How employees request monitors, keyboards, etc.
- [ ] **Internet reimbursement process**: Set up monthly internet reimbursement in payroll
- [ ] **Co-working partnerships**: Establish relationships with co-working providers if offering stipend

### Path B: In-Office Infrastructure

**Week 1-2: Office Space**
- [ ] **Redesign office layout**: Optimize for collaboration (60% collab space, 30% individual, 10% social)
- [ ] **Implement desk system**: Set up hot desking or assign desks, create team neighborhoods
- [ ] **Upgrade meeting rooms**: Ensure video conferencing equipment in all rooms for hybrid meetings
- [ ] **Install badge/tracking**: Implement attendance tracking system

**Week 2-3: Commuter Support**
- [ ] **Set up transit benefits**: Partner with transit providers for subsidized passes
- [ ] **Parking arrangements**: Confirm parking availability/costs, implement allocation system
- [ ] **Commute alternatives**: Bike storage, shower facilities, carpool matching
- [ ] **Flexible hours policy**: Define core hours and flex time parameters

**Week 3-4: Hybrid Meeting Infrastructure**
- [ ] **Test hybrid meeting setup**: Pilot with several teams, ensure remote participants can fully participate
- [ ] **Create hybrid meeting guidelines**: Best practices for inclusive hybrid meetings
- [ ] **Calendar system setup**: Ensure remote vs. office days are visible in calendar
- [ ] **Home office equipment**: Process equipment orders for remote day setups

### Both Paths: Common Tasks

- [ ] **Manager training development**: Finalize curriculum for managing distributed/hybrid teams
- [ ] **Employee training development**: Create async communication or hybrid work training
- [ ] **Security policy updates**: Update security policies for remote work scenarios
- [ ] **VPN capacity**: Ensure VPN can handle all employees remote simultaneously if needed
- [ ] **Metrics automation**: Set up automated data pulls for dashboard (calendar analytics, badge data, etc.)

**Deliverable**: Core infrastructure operational, training materials ready, equipment procurement in progress

## Month 4: Training and Soft Launch

**Goal**: Train employees and managers, pilot with select teams

### Week 1-2: Manager Training

- [ ] **Conduct manager training**: All managers complete remote/hybrid management training (4-6 hours)
  - **Path A topics**: Managing async teams, output-based evaluation, timezone equity, documentation expectations
  - **Path B topics**: Hybrid meeting facilitation, proximity bias awareness, inclusive team culture
- [ ] **Provide manager resources**: Playbooks, templates, 1-on-1 guides
- [ ] **Set up manager support**: Regular office hours or Slack channel for manager questions
- [ ] **Train on bias awareness**: Specific training on proximity bias (Path B) or timezone bias (Path A)

### Week 2-3: Employee Training

- [ ] **Launch employee training**: All employees complete work model training (2-3 hours)
  - **Path A**: Async communication, documentation practices, remote security
  - **Path B**: Hybrid work norms, office etiquette, using remote days effectively
- [ ] **Provide quick reference guides**: One-pagers on key policies (equipment, security, expectations)
- [ ] **Host Q&A sessions**: Department-level sessions to answer questions
- [ ] **Set up ongoing resources**: Wiki page with FAQs, videos, guides

### Week 3-4: Pilot Launch

- [ ] **Select pilot teams**: 2-3 teams representing different functions/locations for pilot
- [ ] **Launch pilot**: Pilot teams operate under new policy for 2-4 weeks
- [ ] **Weekly pilot check-ins**: Gather feedback on what's working, what's not
- [ ] **Iterate based on feedback**: Make adjustments to tools, processes, guidelines
- [ ] **Document learnings**: Create runbook of common issues and solutions

**Deliverable**: Managers and employees trained, pilot complete with documented learnings

---

## Month 5: Full Rollout Preparation

**Goal**: Prepare for company-wide rollout based on pilot learnings

### Week 1: Pilot Review and Adjustment

- [ ] **Analyze pilot feedback**: Synthesize feedback from pilot teams
- [ ] **Update policy/processes**: Refine based on pilot learnings
- [ ] **Update training materials**: Incorporate pilot insights into training
- [ ] **Share pilot results**: Communicate to company what you learned and what changed

### Week 2-3: Rollout Logistics

**Path A specific**:
- [ ] **Complete documentation sprint**: Get critical documentation to 80% complete
- [ ] **Set up in-person gathering schedule**: Plan first all-hands, team offsites, onboarding sessions
- [ ] **Finalize equipment distribution**: Ensure all employees have home office stipends processed
- [ ] **Communication norms posted**: Publish final async communication guidelines

**Path B specific**:
- [ ] **Office readiness**: Ensure all office spaces ready (desks, rooms, amenities)
- [ ] **Publish office schedules**: Communicate anchor days and team-specific requirements
- [ ] **Test attendance tracking**: Ensure badge/booking systems working smoothly
- [ ] **Commute benefits active**: All commuter programs ready for use

**Both paths**:
- [ ] **Exception process live**: Accommodation request workflow operational
- [ ] **Metrics dashboard live**: Share Month 0 baseline with company, explain metrics being tracked
- [ ] **IT support ready**: Help desk trained on new equipment/tool questions

### Week 4: Communication and Launch

- [ ] **Launch date communication**: Announce official policy start date (aim for Month 6 start)
- [ ] **Executive all-hands**: Exec sponsor reinforces why this path, expectations, support available
- [ ] **Department-level rollout meetings**: Each dept meets to discuss how policy applies to them
- [ ] **Launch celebration**: Mark the occasion positively (not as "end of flexibility" but as "intentional future")

**Deliverable**: Company ready for full policy launch on Month 6 Day 1

## Month 6: Full Launch

**Goal**: Policy goes live company-wide with support and monitoring

### Week 1-2: Launch Support

- [ ] **Policy goes live**: All employees operating under new policy
- [ ] **Daily monitoring**: Watch for issues (technical problems, confusion, compliance)
- [ ] **Rapid response**: Address blockers immediately (e.g., equipment delays, tool access issues)
- [ ] **Manager support office hours**: Daily drop-in sessions for managers navigating questions
- [ ] **Employee help channel**: Active monitoring of questions/concerns

### Week 2-4: Stabilization

- [ ] **Track initial metrics**: Begin collecting data for success metrics
  - **Path A**: Documentation usage, meeting time, remote engagement
  - **Path B**: Office utilization, proximity bias indicators, collaboration satisfaction
- [ ] **Address early issues**: Common problems get documented solutions
- [ ] **Share early wins**: Highlight positive examples of policy working well
- [ ] **Collect feedback**: Pulse survey on first-month experience

**Path B specific**:
- [ ] **Monitor office utilization**: Daily tracking of anchor day attendance
- [ ] **Address attendance issues**: Follow up with employees missing office requirements
- [ ] **Optimize space**: Adjust desk allocations based on actual usage patterns

**Path A specific**:
- [ ] **Documentation health check**: Review documentation usage and currency
- [ ] **Async norm monitoring**: Are meetings decreasing? Is documentation being created?
- [ ] **Timezone equity check**: Early scan for timezone disadvantage patterns

### Week 4: One-Month Retrospective

- [ ] **Gather feedback**: What's working? What's not? What's surprising?
- [ ] **Analyze metrics**: Review first month of data against targets
- [ ] **Identify adjustments**: What needs to change based on first month?
- [ ] **Communicate learnings**: Share feedback and actions with company transparently

**Deliverable**: Policy live and stabilized, Month 1 metrics captured, initial adjustments identified

## Months 7-9: Measurement and Iteration

**Goal**: Track metrics, make data-driven adjustments, build maturity

### Month 7

- [ ] **Collect Q2 engagement data**: First full engagement survey under new policy
- [ ] **Analyze engagement by segment**: Look for disparities by location, demographics, etc.
- [ ] **Manager feedback session**: How is policy working from manager perspective?
- [ ] **Equipment audit**: Are employees getting what they need? Any gaps?
- [ ] **Metric dashboard update**: Publish Month 7 metrics publicly

**Path A specific**:
- [ ] **Documentation audit**: Check % of docs updated in last 6 months
- [ ] **Meeting time analysis**: Has avg meeting time decreased? Who's still overloaded?
- [ ] **First in-person gathering**: Execute first team offsites or all-hands

**Path B specific**:
- [ ] **Promotion cycle review**: First promotion cycle under new policy—check for proximity bias
- [ ] **Office experience survey**: How do employees rate the office environment?
- [ ] **Utilization analysis**: Are anchor days working? Should they change?

### Month 8

- [ ] **Address identified gaps**: Work on issues surfaced in Month 7 review
- [ ] **Training reinforcement**: Refresher sessions on common struggles (async communication, hybrid meetings, etc.)
- [ ] **Policy clarifications**: Update FAQ based on recurring questions
- [ ] **Cross-team learning**: Share best practices from teams that are thriving

**Path A specific**:
- [ ] **Async champions program**: Identify and empower employees who excel at async work
- [ ] **Documentation sprint**: Push to get remaining critical docs to 80%+ complete
- [ ] **Timezone analysis**: Review meeting schedules for timezone equity

**Path B specific**:
- [ ] **Proximity bias training refresh**: Reinforce with managers showing concerning patterns
- [ ] **Long commuter check-in**: Survey employees with >60 min commutes on policy impact
- [ ] **Office amenity feedback**: What would make office time more valuable?

### Month 9

- [ ] **Collect Q3 engagement data**: Second quarterly engagement survey
- [ ] **Compare trends**: Are engagement, satisfaction improving, stable, or declining?
- [ ] **Attrition analysis**: Review departures in first 9 months for patterns
- [ ] **Success stories documentation**: Capture and share examples of policy working well
- [ ] **Metric dashboard update**: Publish Month 9 metrics with trend analysis

**3-Quarter review**:
- [ ] **Assess against success metrics**: Are you on track to hit 12-month targets?
- [ ] **Identify risks**: Any concerning trends that need intervention?
- [ ] **Adjust course**: Make changes if data shows policy isn't working as intended
- [ ] **Communicate status**: Transparent update to company on how policy is performing

**Deliverable**: 9-month progress assessment, course corrections implemented

## Months 10-12: Refinement and Annual Review

**Goal**: Finalize first-year performance, prepare for annual review and year 2

### Month 10

- [ ] **Begin annual review prep**: Start compiling data for comprehensive 12-month review
- [ ] **Employee focus groups**: Conduct qualitative sessions with diverse employee groups
- [ ] **Manager survey**: Structured survey on what's working and what needs to change
- [ ] **Cost analysis**: Calculate total costs (infrastructure, equipment, office space) vs. benefits
- [ ] **Competitive analysis**: How does our policy compare to competitors? Impact on hiring?

### Month 11

- [ ] **Collect Q4 engagement data**: Final quarterly engagement survey of year 1
- [ ] **Promotion cycle analysis**: Year-end promotions—check for equity across locations/attendance
- [ ] **Compile success metrics**: Pull together all 6 metrics for 12-month assessment
- [ ] **Draft annual review report**: Synthesize quantitative and qualitative data
- [ ] **Identify year 2 priorities**: Based on year 1 data, what should year 2 focus on?

### Month 12

- [ ] **Complete 12-month comprehensive review**: Use template in success metrics document
  - [ ] All 6 metrics vs. targets
  - [ ] Qualitative assessment (employee/manager feedback)
  - [ ] Cost-benefit analysis
  - [ ] Decision: Continue, adjust, or change course
- [ ] **Leadership review session**: Present findings to executive team, make recommendations
- [ ] **Decide on year 2 changes**: Any policy adjustments based on data?
- [ ] **Communicate results**: Transparent all-hands on year 1 performance and year 2 plans
- [ ] **Update policy document**: Incorporate any changes into policy v2.0
- [ ] **Celebrate wins**: Acknowledge what went well and thank employees for adapting

**Annual review should answer**:
- ✅ Did we meet our success criteria? (5-6 metrics met = success)
- ✅ Is this policy serving our business and employees?
- ✅ What needs to change for year 2?
- ✅ Should we continue this path or pivot?

**Deliverable**: Comprehensive annual review, year 2 plan, updated policy (if needed)

## Ongoing: Post-Month 12

After the first 12 months, establish ongoing rhythms:

### Monthly
- [ ] **Metrics dashboard update**: Keep metrics current and visible
- [ ] **Support office hours**: Regular sessions for questions/issues
- [ ] **Policy updates**: Address edge cases and clarifications as they arise

### Quarterly
- [ ] **Engagement survey**: Continue quarterly pulse checks
- [ ] **Equity review**: Check promotion, compensation, attrition by relevant segments
- [ ] **Metrics review session**: Leadership team reviews trends and decides on actions
- [ ] **Communication**: Share quarterly metrics and actions with company

### Annually
- [ ] **Comprehensive review**: Full assessment against success metrics
- [ ] **Policy refresh**: Update policy based on learnings
- [ ] **Retake capability assessment**: Has your capability evolved? Should your path?
- [ ] **Strategic alignment check**: Does policy still serve business strategy?

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### Don't Skip Baseline Measurement
**Problem**: Without Month 0 data, you can't know if things improved or declined.  
**Solution**: Capture baseline before policy launch even if imperfect.

### Don't Under-Invest in Training
**Problem**: Employees and managers struggle without proper training, policy fails.  
**Solution**: Budget adequate time and resources for comprehensive training.

### Don't Ignore Early Warning Signs
**Problem**: Small issues compound; by Month 6 you have big problems.  
**Solution**: Monitor metrics monthly, address issues immediately.

### Don't Make It Optional
**Problem**: If policy is "optional" or "pilot," people won't adapt and it won't work.  
**Solution**: Clear launch date, clear expectations, clear accountability.

### Don't Fail to Communicate
**Problem**: Employees don't understand why policy exists, feel it's arbitrary.  
**Solution**: Transparent communication about rationale, data, trade-offs.

### Don't Ignore Equity Metrics
**Problem**: Proximity bias or other inequities create resentment and attrition.  
**Solution**: Monitor and address equity metrics as seriously as business metrics.

## Checklist Customization

This checklist is comprehensive but should be customized:

- **Adjust timeline**: Smaller orgs may move faster; larger orgs may need more time
- **Add company-specific tasks**: Industry regulations, union negotiations, etc.
- **Remove irrelevant tasks**: If you don't have offices, skip office design tasks
- **Assign owners**: Add owner names to each task for accountability
