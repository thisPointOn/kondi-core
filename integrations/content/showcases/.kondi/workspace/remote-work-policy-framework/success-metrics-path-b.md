# Success Metrics: Path B (In-Office Requirement)

## Purpose

This document defines measurable success criteria for in-office requirement policy implementation. These metrics help you determine whether your in-office policy is working or needs revision.

**Review Cadence**: Monthly data collection, quarterly assessment, annual comprehensive review

**Accountability**: [People/HR Team] owns data collection and reporting; [Executive Sponsor] owns action on failure indicators

---

## 12-Month Acceptance Criteria

Your in-office policy is successful if, after 12 months, you meet the following targets:

### Metric 1: Employee Engagement and Satisfaction

**What to measure**: Overall employee engagement and specific satisfaction with work arrangement

**Success target**:
- Overall engagement score ≥[70/100 or 4/5]
- Work arrangement satisfaction ≥65% (employees satisfied with office requirement)
- Engagement trajectory stable or improving over 12 months
- Engagement comparable to pre-policy baseline (if applicable)

**Why it matters**: In-office requirements only work if employees believe they serve a purpose. Sustained low satisfaction indicates the policy is damaging morale.

**Data collection method**:
- Quarterly engagement survey with overall engagement questions
- Specific questions: "The office requirement helps me do my best work" (% agree)
- "I can balance work and personal life with current office requirements" (% agree)
- Segment by: Department, role type, commute distance, demographics

**Failure indicator**: If overall engagement <60 or work arrangement satisfaction <50% for 2 consecutive quarters.

**Sample tracking template**:

| Quarter | Overall Engagement | Work Arrangement Satisfaction | Office Requirement Helpful | Work-Life Balance |
|---------|-------------------|------------------------------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| Q1 | 71 | 67% | 62% | 68% |
| Q2 | 72 | 68% | 65% | 70% |
| Q3 | | | | |
| Q4 | | | | |

**Analysis note**: Look for patterns—e.g., if long commuters rate significantly lower, consider office location strategy.

---

### Metric 2: Proximity Bias Monitoring

**What to measure**: Promotion rates and compensation changes by office attendance level

**Success target**:
- Employees meeting minimum office requirement (e.g., 3 days) promoted at same rate as those exceeding requirement (e.g., 4-5 days)
- Promotion rate gap <5 percentage points between minimum and maximum office attendance
- Compensation increases within ±3 percentage points
- Performance ratings distributed similarly regardless of office attendance level

**Why it matters**: This is the critical equity metric for Path B. In-office policies create proximity bias risk; active monitoring is essential.

**Data collection method**:
- Track all promotions, compensation changes, performance ratings
- Segment by: Office days per week (minimum required vs. above minimum), commute distance, department
- Calculate: (# promoted / # eligible) for each segment

**Failure indicator**: If employees at minimum office requirement promoted at rate >5 points below higher-attendance employees for 2 consecutive cycles.

**Sample tracking template**:

| Office Attendance | Eligible Employees | Promoted | Promotion Rate | Avg Comp Increase | Avg Performance Rating |
|------------------|-------------------|----------|----------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| 3 days (minimum) | 145 | 22 | 15.2% | 6.1% | 3.7/5 |
| 4 days | 118 | 19 | 16.1% | 6.3% | 3.8/5 |
| 5 days | 65 | 11 | 16.9% | 6.5% | 3.9/5 |

**Analysis**: Promotion rate gap 1.7 points (✓), compensation gap 0.4 points (✓), rating gap minimal (✓). Meeting target.

**Warning sign**: If there's a linear correlation between office days and outcomes, proximity bias is occurring.

---

### Metric 3: Office Utilization and Space Efficiency

**What to measure**: Office space usage relative to capacity and design

**Success target**:
- Office attendance on anchor days ≥85% of expected attendance
- Desk/space utilization 60-80% on anchor days (not overcrowded, not empty)
- <10% of employees consistently missing office requirements without accommodations
- Meeting room booking rate 50-70% (rooms are used but available when needed)

**Why it matters**: If office is empty, the policy isn't justified. If overcrowded, the experience is poor. Utilization validates the investment.

**Data collection method**:
- Badge swipe data or desk booking system
- Calculate: Daily attendance / total employees required to be in office
- Meeting room booking analytics
- Spot checks on anchor days (visual observation + badge data)

**Failure indicator**: If office utilization <60% on anchor days consistently, the policy isn't serving its purpose.

**Sample tracking template**:

| Week | Mon % | Tue % (Anchor) | Wed % | Thu % (Anchor) | Fri % | Avg Anchor Day % | Meeting Room Booking % |
|------|-------|---------------|-------|---------------|-------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Week 1 | 52% | 87% | 61% | 89% | 38% | 88% | 64% |
| Week 2 | 48% | 85% | 58% | 86% | 35% | 85.5% | 61% |
| Week 3 | 51% | 88% | 63% | 90% | 41% | 89% | 67% |
| Week 4 | | | | | | | |

**Target**: Anchor days consistently 85%+, non-anchor days lower (expected), meeting rooms well-used

### Metric 4: Hiring and Talent Access

**What to measure**: Time-to-fill roles, offer acceptance rates, quality of hire

**Success target**:
- Time-to-fill within [industry benchmark or baseline] (e.g., ≤60 days for most roles)
- Offer acceptance rate ≥70% (candidates accept offers)
- New hire quality meets expectations (manager rating at 90 days)
- Diverse candidate pipeline (hitting diversity hiring goals)

**Why it matters**: In-office requirements constrain geographic hiring. Success requires effective local talent access. If you can't hire quality candidates, the constraint is too limiting.

**Data collection method**:
- Track time-to-fill from req open to offer accepted
- Offer acceptance rate: (offers accepted / offers extended)
- New hire effectiveness: Manager rating at 90 days "This hire is meeting/exceeding expectations"
- Pipeline diversity: % diverse candidates at each stage vs. goals

**Failure indicator**: If time-to-fill >90 days for multiple critical roles, or if offer acceptance <60%, geographic constraint is problematic.

**Sample tracking template**:

| Quarter | Avg Time-to-Fill (days) | Offer Acceptance Rate | New Hire Effectiveness (% Meeting+) | Pipeline Diversity vs. Goal |
|---------|------------------------|---------------------|-------------------------------------|----------------------------|
| Q1 | 58 | 72% | 87% | -5% (below goal) |
| Q2 | 54 | 74% | 91% | -2% (below goal) |
| Q3 | | | | |
| Q4 | | | | |

**Action trigger**: If consistently missing diversity goals, consider whether office location limits diverse candidate access.

### Metric 5: Voluntary Attrition by Demographic and Commute

**What to measure**: Resignation rates segmented by commute distance, demographics, and tenure

**Success target**:
- Overall attrition ≤[15%] annually (adjust for industry)
- Attrition within ±5 percentage points across commute distances (short vs. long commute)
- No demographic group with attrition >10 points above company average
- Exit interview data shows <20% cite office requirement as primary departure reason

**Why it matters**: If employees with long commutes or specific demographics leave at higher rates, the policy is inequitable. If office requirement is top exit reason, policy is failing.

**Data collection method**:
- Track all voluntary departures
- Segment by: Commute distance, demographic groups, tenure, department, office attendance pattern
- Exit interview coding: Was office requirement a factor? Primary/secondary/not a factor

**Failure indicator**: If long-commute employees (>45 min) have attrition >8 points above short-commute, or if >30% of exits cite office requirement.

**Sample tracking template**:

| Segment | Headcount | Departures (12mo) | Attrition Rate | Office Requirement as Exit Factor |
|---------|-----------|------------------|----------------|----------------------------------|
| Overall | 328 | 44 | 13.4% | 24% (primary/secondary) |
| Commute <30min | 178 | 21 | 11.8% | 18% |
| Commute 30-60min | 115 | 17 | 14.8% | 28% |
| Commute >60min | 35 | 6 | 17.1% | 42% |
| Underrepresented Groups | 94 | 15 | 16.0% | 31% |

**Analysis**: Long commute and underrepresented groups show higher attrition and office requirement as factor—investigate accommodations and equity.

---

### Metric 6: Collaboration and Productivity Indicators

**What to measure**: Indicators that office presence is delivering intended collaboration benefits

**Success target**:
- ≥70% of employees agree "I can collaborate effectively with my team"
- ≥65% agree "Being in office helps me do better work"
- Cross-functional project completion on time ≥80%
- Manager assessment: Team collaboration is strong (≥4/5 rating)

**Why it matters**: In-office policy is justified by collaboration benefits. If employees don't perceive these benefits, the policy is costly without payoff.

**Data collection method**:
- Quarterly survey questions on collaboration effectiveness
- Project management data: % projects completed on time, by team
- Manager survey: "My team collaborates effectively" (quarterly)
- Spot question: "What work would be harder if you were fully remote?" (qualitative)

**Failure indicator**: If <60% see collaboration benefits or manager ratings <3.5/5, the office experience isn't delivering value.

**Sample tracking template**:

| Quarter | Collaboration Effective | Office Helps Work | Projects On-Time | Manager Collaboration Rating |
|---------|------------------------|------------------|------------------|----------------------------|
| Q1 | 68% | 62% | 78% | 3.9/5 |
| Q2 | 71% | 66% | 82% | 4.1/5 |
| Q3 | | | | |
| Q4 | | | | |

**Qualitative insight**: Track themes in "what would be harder remote" to understand perceived value.

## Monthly Tracking Cadence

**What to track monthly**:
1. Office utilization (badge swipes/desk bookings)
2. Attrition by segment (update running 12-month total)
3. Hiring funnel metrics (time-to-fill, offer acceptance)

**What to track quarterly**:
1. Engagement survey (overall + work arrangement satisfaction)
2. Promotion and compensation analysis (after each cycle)
3. Collaboration effectiveness (survey questions)
4. Manager feedback on team performance

**What to track annually**:
1. Comprehensive 12-month assessment against all 6 metrics
2. Cost analysis (office space costs vs. productivity gains)
3. Competitive positioning (are we losing talent to remote-first competitors?)

## Failure Indicators: When to Review Policy

If ANY of the following occur, convene policy review immediately:

### Red Flags (Immediate Review)

1. **Systematic proximity bias**: Employees at minimum office days promoted/compensated at >5 points lower rate for 2 consecutive cycles
2. **Engagement collapse**: Overall engagement drops >10 points or satisfaction with work arrangement <50%
3. **Attrition spike**: Overall attrition >20% annually or long-commute attrition >20 points above short-commute
4. **Hiring failure**: Unable to fill critical roles within 90 days consistently, or offer acceptance <50%
5. **Exodus risk**: Multiple high performers request remote accommodations or resign citing office requirement
6. **Utilization failure**: Office utilization <50% on anchor days for 3 consecutive months

### Yellow Flags (Investigate)

1. Specific demographic groups consistently showing lower engagement or higher attrition
2. Office requirement cited in >30% of exit interviews
3. Commute-related equity gaps (long commuters disadvantaged)
4. Low perceived collaboration value (<60% say office helps work)
5. Manager feedback indicating office requirement creates hiring obstacles

**Review process**:
1. Convene stakeholders (exec sponsor, HR, sample of employees including long commuters)
2. Analyze root cause data
3. Options: Adjust requirements (reduce days, change anchor days, expand accommodations), improve office experience, or pivot to Path A
4. Communicate decision and rationale transparently

- **Process**: All voluntary departures complete exit interview

- **Analysis**: Code themes quarterly; track % citing office requirement as factor

### Hiring Metrics
- **Source**: [ATS - Greenhouse / Lever / Workday Recruiting]
- **Metrics**: Time-to-fill, offer acceptance rate, candidate pipeline diversity
- **Cadence**: Monthly reporting, quarterly analysis

## Benchmarking and Targets

Set targets based on:

1. **Your baseline**: Pre-policy metrics (if changing from different model)
2. **Industry benchmarks**: Engagement, attrition, time-to-hire for your industry
3. **Local market**: Commute norms and office prevalence in your metro area

**Example target-setting**:
- If pre-policy engagement was 72/100 → Target: Maintain ≥70 (don't drop >2 points)
- If industry attrition is 18% → Target: ≤18% (don't exceed industry)
- If metro area avg commute is 35 min → Set commute distance parameters accordingly

**Adjust for context**: If you're in a market where most companies are remote-first, you may face higher attrition; adjust targets and recruitment strategies.

## 12-Month Comprehensive Review

At 12 months, conduct comprehensive review:

### Quantitative Assessment
- All 6 metrics vs. targets (table above)
- Cost analysis: Office space costs, commuter benefits vs. collaboration/productivity benefits
- Competitive impact: Lost candidates to remote-first competitors

### Qualitative Assessment
- Employee feedback themes (surveys, focus groups, town halls)
- Manager feedback on team effectiveness and hiring
- Executive assessment: Does in-office serve business strategy?

### Decision Points

**If meeting 5-6 metrics**: In-office policy is working. Continue with iterative improvement.

**If meeting 3-4 metrics**: Partially working. Options:
- Reduce office days requirement (e.g., from 4 to 3 days)
- Expand accommodation eligibility
- Open satellite offices in underserved areas
- Improve office experience (better space design, amenities)

**If meeting 0-2 metrics**: Policy is failing. Options:
1. Significant changes (reduce to 2 days, make remote days majority)
2. Pivot to Path A (remote-first)
3. Hybrid approach (team-level decisions rather than company-wide)

**Communication**: Share review transparently. If changing policy, explain what data drove the decision.

## Addressing Common Issues

### Issue: Long-Commute Attrition High

**Investigate**:
- Are long commuters concentrated in specific demographics?
- Did they have long commutes at hire, or did circumstances change?
- Are there patterns (e.g., parents with long commutes)?

**Potential solutions**:
- Expand remote accommodation eligibility for >60 min commutes
- Open satellite office/co-working arrangements
- Adjust anchor days to reduce commute frequency
- Provide commuter benefits (transit stipends, parking, flexible hours)

### Issue: Proximity Bias Detected

**Investigate**:
- Which managers show the pattern? (May be specific to certain leaders)
- Is it conscious or unconscious bias?
- Are evaluation criteria clear and output-based?

**Potential solutions**:
- Manager training on bias in hybrid environments
- Calibration sessions to review promotion decisions for bias
- Anonymous promotion packets (remove attendance data from review)
- Explicit policy: Office attendance beyond minimum cannot be promotion factor

### Issue: Office Utilization Low

**Investigate**:
- Are specific days low (e.g., Fridays always empty)?
- Are people complying but office feels empty (bad design)?
- Is enforcement inconsistent?

**Potential solutions**:
- Adjust anchor days based on actual team needs
- Redesign space to concentrate people (don't spread across empty floor)
- Improve enforcement (if compliance is the issue)
- Reduce requirement if office isn't serving its purpose

### Issue: Can't Hire Quality Candidates

**Investigate**:
- Is geographic constraint too limiting?
- Are competitors offering remote?
- Is compensation competitive?
- Are specific roles struggling (e.g., engineering)?

**Potential solutions**:
- Make certain hard-to-fill roles remote-eligible
- Open office in new geography
- Increase compensation to offset office requirement
- Temporary remote onboarding (first 3-6 months) to access broader talent
